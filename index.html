    What We Do

    Welcome Home

    We give each interpreter and their family a hero's welcome at the airport, then take them to their home, which we've helped secure and fully furnish.

    Employment Assistance

    We provide resume reviews, help with networking, interviewing, and application skills, and job-search guidance.

    Social Networking

    Incoming families often face isolation and unease as they transition. We help them engage with each other and the community through social and networking events.

    First Friends

    For every incoming family, we have dedicated volunteers that will become their first friends in their new life here, and help forge them into great citizens of Pittsburgh and the U.S.

    Transportation Assistance

    Wherever our families need to get, we get them there, teaching them the ropes for using public transportation, giving them rides, and as cars are donated, families have a chance to earn them through service to the community.

    Education and Training

    Every day, we add more partners who train and prepare our interpreters and their family to be competitive in the workforce, and to acquire and improve language and communication skills. We also connect interested candidates to tradecraft training and industry specific schools.

    First Friend

    The most important service our organization provides is the First Friend. Each family or interpreter we bring in needs a guide to help them transition to a new country and culture. Most of the time, this is as simple as answering questions about paperwork, helping them navigate a bureaucratic system, help enrolling the children in school, or just advice about how to handle something. It's a friendship. The connection our First Friends make with their family is incredibly strong, and most often looks and feels like any friendship you've ever had. It's incredibly rewarding, a lifechanging experience. As with all our volunteers, we provide all the training you'll need, and there are no contractual requirements.

    General Volunteer

    Most of the work our volunteers do is on request. Every day we receive requests from families for help with paperwork for bills, signing kids up for sports, help finding a good market in their area, almost anything you can think of. Often, the First Friends for that family provide direct help, but it's a team effort, and for those that want to be involved but need more flexibility than being a First Friend allows, this is the perfect option.

    Event Planning Assistant

    We try to have a minimum of one event each month, in addition to our monthly scheduled pot-luck dinners. Sporting events, family outings, museum days, service projects...the only limit is your imagination, and we need your ideas. Then we need your planning help to make your ideas a huge, successful reality!

    Salesforce Administrator

    Recently, No One Left Behind has adopted Salesforce as our CRM platform. This helps us track our families, volunteers, and events, and makes reporting more manageable. The job is mostly data entry, and no direct experience with Salesforce is required, but general computer knowledge is helpful.

    Social Media Assistant

    The organization has Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram accounts, but we are barely posting 10% of the good work we do, and we're constantly missing out on opportunities to reach out to new volunteers, potential employers and landlords, and partners in general. We need a small team of volunteers to post a couple times a week with simple updates, pictures, and links to content that will expand our reach.

    Donation Pick Up Team

    It's a thankless job, but our success in setting up incoming families is entirely predicated on our ability to outfit their home with everything you would hope to find in your own home. We rely almost entirely on donated items to fill this requirement, but that means we need hands to help pick up and drop off these items. We need a pool of volunteers who can occasionally lend a hand.

    Resume Review Volunteer

    One of the greatest challenges facing our families is adapting their experiences for inclusion in a resume. While most speak very good English, the task of shortening blurbs and tailoring language for the job market is a skill they need to help them succeed and get their feet under them with a good job.

    Webdesign Assistant

    If it's not already clear from the state of our site, we always need help from volunteers to maintain and update our site content. This doesn't necessarily require knowledge of web design, just a desire to learn.

    Welcome Home

    We give each interpreter and their family a hero's welcome at the airport, then take them to their residence, which we help secure and outfit with the donated items, everything needed to make it a home.

    Employment Assistance

    We are always working to cultivate partnerships with area employers to provide that initial job upon arrival. Once the family is secure, we continue by working with each adult to develop career goals, and help them achieve those goals.

    Social Networking

    We hold monthly social events to keep our growing family in contact. Whether it's a pot luck dinner at the Islamic Center, a day in Schenley Park, or a trip to the zoo, we are always looking for ways to provide social opportunities to our families, and just as important, our volunteers.

    First Friends

    One of the most important roles in our organization are our First Friends. It's also the most rewarding. While the organization picks up all the work of helping stand the family up in a new city, it's the First Friend that will help them navigate their new world, and become true Pittsburghers. Many times it's as simple as taking them out to dinner, or answering questions about a holiday or tradition, but often the First Friend is a life line, the first person the family calls if there is a problem or issue they don't understand. It's a vital role, and most of the time, the First Friend becomes a life-long friend, and key person in the lives of the family and their children.

    Transportation Assistance

    Transportation is a vital need for our families. It's the key to employment early on, and to freedom for the family. We and our partners help provide initial training for navigating the city by public transportation, often providing volunteers for ride-alongs early in the process. We also have a national partnership with Lyft to provide emergency transportation as necessary. Finally, we are beginning to gain momentum in car donations, which we service and gift to families that volunteer with us to help their fellow SIV families.

    Education and Training

    As with any American, our families want and need opportunities to train and learn skills that fit the needs or our local economy. Their children are the future of our local economy. We constantly seek opportunities for them to train or learn, through programs at local colleges, the Trade Institute and local Unions, programs offered through the Carnegie Libraries and Museums, and the many excellent partners in the immigrant space here in Pittsburgh.
